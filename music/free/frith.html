Frith

Setting Langston Hughes's long poem "Freedom's Plow" to music back in 2002-2003 piqued my interest in recording a set of more-or-less political songs. I say more-or-less because freedom is about much more than politics: it can be personal, artistic, educational, even viscerally physical. Furthermore, I see freedom as deeply connected to family, friendship, and community (to explain why would require a complete essay unto itself -- liner notes, anyone?).

I've written a handful of tunes that might fit into such a collection, and I cover a handful more. Adding in "Freedom's Plow" (which is over 25 minutes long) could result in a powerful collection of songs about the importance of freedom. Here is a possible lineup:

  • All Along the Watchtower (Bob Dylan)
  • Find the Cost of Freedom / Woodstock (CSNY / Joni Mitchell)
  • The Ballad of Ego Brown
  • Peter and Paul
  • Taxman (George Harrison)
  • Sleep Come Free Me (James Taylor)
  • Sunshine (Jonathan Edwards)
  • Redemption Song (Bob Marley)
  • It's All Right Ma (Bob Dylan)
  • The Times They Are A-Changin' (Bob Dylan)
  • Freedom's Plow (words by Langston Hughes)
  • The Course of the Sun
