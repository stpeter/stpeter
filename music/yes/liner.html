Fundamental Affirmation

To Be Over

It's fitting to begin with the strains of the last, quiet notes from Chris Squire's solo album Fish Out of Water, which seque perfectly into melody of To Be Over, one of the gentlest of Yes songs. I had a lot of fun exploring the contrapuntal interplay of Chris Squire's bass, Steve Howe's guitar, and Patrick Moraz's keyboards.

South Side of the Sky

Oftentimes, Yes music is a study in contrasts. Here we are cast from the tranquil scene of floating down a calming stream to the death of a group of friends in a howling mountain blizzard. I decided to turn the song inside out by starting with the piano interlude from the middle and taking my time to get around to the verse.

Turn of the Century

As Chris Squire does on this beautiful song from Going For the One, I mostly follow the melody line here. To emphasize the sense of lamentation in the original, I take the song at a slightly slower pace than Yes do.

Tales from Topographic Oceans

Distilling the 80 minutes of Tales from Topographic Oceans into a short suite might seem like an impossible task, but there's a precedent: Steve Howe did it on his 1993 album Not Necessarily Acoustic. Although I've chosen different themes from several of the movements, Howe's performance provided a great frame of reference.

Tech Notes

Recorded direct to a MacBook Pro through a Bass Buddy preamp and M-Audio USB interface using Audacity software. The instrument is a four-string, 24-fret Minotaur bass wired with a piezo pickup at the bridge and strung with LaBella CB40 nylon-core strings.
