Fundamental Affirmation

This collection was inspired by my reading of Bill Martin's book Music of Yes and conceived as something like a modern equivalent of Bach's Cello Suites. Over time, I have come to see deeper musical parallels to the six movements of a baroque suite (prelude, allemande, courante, sarabande, bourrée, gigue).

To Be Over

It's fitting to begin with the strains of the last, quiet notes from Chris Squire's solo album Fish Out of Water, which segue perfectly into the melody of To Be Over, one of the gentlest of Yes songs. In the free, almost improvisatory style of a prelude, I explore the contrapuntal interplay of Chris Squire's bass, Steve Howe's guitar, and Patrick Moraz's keyboards.

South Side of the Sky

Oftentimes, Yes music is a study in contrasts. Here we are cast from the tranquil scene of floating down a calming stream to the stark death of a group of friends in a howling mountain blizzard. In addition to echoing a traditional allemande by including heavy arpeggiation, I decided to turn the song inside out by starting with the piano interlude from the middle section and taking my time to get around to the verse.

Tales from Topographic Oceans

Distilling the four majestic movements of Tales from Topographic Oceans into a short suite for solo bass might seem like an impossible task, but there's a precedent: Steve Howe did it for guitar and voice on his 1993 album Not Necessarily Acoustic. Although I've chosen different themes from several of the movements, Howe's performance provided a great frame of reference for me.

Turn of the Century

As Chris Squire does on this beautiful song from Going For the One, I mostly follow the melody line. To emphasize the sense of lamentation in the original, I take the song at a slightly slower pace than Yes do, turning it into something of a sarabande.

A Venture

This song from The Yes Album is one of the hidden gems of Yes music. Although the words are somewhat serious, the music is easygoing and relaxed in the spirit of a bourrée, even verging on jazz in Steve Howe's guitar solo toward the end of the song.

Close to the Edge

Much Yes music is great and sublime, but Close to the Edge is perfect. Attempting to represent that perfection on a single instrument, especially one as overtly simple as the four-string bass, was a daunting task. Although I am not completely satisfied with this transcription, I did the best I could.

Tech Notes

The instrument is a four-string, 24-fret Minotaur bass wired with a piezo pickup at the bridge and strung with D'Addario nylon tapewound strings. Recorded direct to a System76 laptop through a Bass Buddy preamp and M-Audio USB interface using Audacity software on Ubuntu Linux.
