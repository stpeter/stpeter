Aristotle's Ethical Concepts

Here are my proposed translations for key concepts in Aristotle's thinking about character, action, and wisdom (contrasted with some common, often Latinate renderings):

  • ἀκρασία (akrasia) = lack of self-control (not "incontinence")
  • ἁμαρτία (hamartia) = missing the mark (not "sin")
  • ἀρετή (arete) = thriving, excellence (not "virtue")
  • βίος (bios) = way of life
  • βούλησις (boulesis) = decision, purpose (not "will")
  • διάνοια (dianoia) = thinking (not "intellection")
  • δύναμις (dunamis) = power, strength (not "potentiality")
  • ἐγκράτεια (enkrateia) = self-control (not "continence")
  • εῖδος (eidos) = way of being, shape, form (not "species")
  • ἐνέργεια (energeia) = working at a task (not "actuality")
  • ἐντελέχεια (entelecheia) = achieving a goal, becoming complete (not "actuality")
  • ἐπιθυμία (epithumia) = desire
  • ἔργον (ergon) = task (not "function")
  • εὐδαιμονία (eudaimonia) = flourishing, or living well and doing well (not "happiness")
  • ἕξις (hexis) = settled state, behavior, practice (not "habit")
  • ἦθος (ethos) = character
  • θεωρία (theoria) = inquiry (not "contemplation")
  • θυμός (thumos) = emotion, feeling
  • ὄρεξις (orexis) = reaching, yearning, striving (not "appetition")
  • μεσότης (mesotes) = balance (not "the mean")
  • προαίρεσις (proairesis) = planning, choice
  • σκοπός (skopos) = target
  • σοφία (sophia) = sagacity, wisdom
  • σωφροσύνη (sophrosune) = mindfulness, moderation
  • τέλος (telos) = goal, completeness
  • φρόνησις (phronesis) = wisdom, good judgment (not "prudence")
  • ψυχή (psuche) = aliveness (not "soul")
