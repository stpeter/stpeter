Aristotle's Ethical Concepts

Here are my proposed translations for key concepts in Aristotle's thinking about character, action, and wisdom (contrasted with some common, often Latinate renderings):

  • ἀγαθός (agathos) = good [opposed to κακός]
  • αἵρεσις (hairesis) = pursuit, choice
  • ἀκολασία (akolasia) = decadence, lack of moderation [opposed to σωφροσύνη]
  • ἀκρασία (akrasia) = self-indulgence, lack of self-control (not "incontinence")
  • ἁμαρτία (hamartia) = missing the mark (not "sin")
  • ἀνόητος (anoetos) = thoughtless, lacking in insight
  • ἀπορία (aporia) = impasse, perplexity (not "puzzle")
  • ἀρετή (arete) = thriving, excellence (not "virtue") [opposed to μοχθηρία]
  • ἀφροσύνη (aphrosune) = foolishness [opposed to σωφροσύνη]
  • βίος (bios) = way of life
  • βουλεύεσθαι (bouleuesthai) = to take counsel with oneself, to reflect
  • βούλησις (boulesis) = wish, intention, decision (not "conation")
  • διάνοια (dianoia) = thinking (not "intellection")
  • δύναμις (dunamis) = ability, capacity (not "potentiality")
  • ἐγκράτεια (enkrateia) = self-control (not "continence")
  • εἶδος (eidos) = way of being, identity (not "species" or "form")
  • ἐνέργεια (energeia) = working at a task (not "activity")
  • ἐντελέχεια (entelecheia) = achieving a goal, becoming complete (not "actuality")
  • ἕξις (hexis) = practice (not "habit")
  • ἐπιθυμία (epithumia) = craving, need
  • ἔργον (ergon) = task (not "function")
  • εὐβουλία (euboulia) = success at reflection; deliberating well
  • εὐδαιμονία (eudaimonia) = flourishing, living well (not "happiness")
  • εὐπραξία (eupraxia) = success at taking action; conducting oneself well
  • ἡδύ (hedu) = enjoyment, pleasure
  • ἦθος (ethos) = character
  • θεωρία (theoria) = inquiry (not "contemplation")
  • θυμός (thumos) = feeling, want
  • κακός (kakos) = bad [opposed to ἀγαθός]
  • καλόν (kalon) = rightness, beauty (not "nobility")
  • λογ ίζομαι (logizomai) = to consider, to reflect (not "ratiocinate")
  • μεγαλοψυχία (megalopsuchia) = greatness of soul (not "pride")
  • μεσότης (mesotes) = balance (not "the mean")
  • μοχθηρία (mochtheria) = corruption [opposed to ἀρετή]
  • νοῦς (nous) = insight (not "intellect")
  • ὄρεξις (orexis) = reaching, yearning (not "appetition")
  • πάθος (pathos) = experience; feeling
  • πράξις (praxis) = taking action
  • προαίρεσις (proairesis) = forechoice, planning, decision-making
  • σκοπός (skopos) = target
  • σοφία (sophia) = sagacity, wisdom
  • σοφός (sophos) = knowing, sagacious
  • σπουδαῖος (spoudaios) = worthy, serious, good [opposed to φαῦλος]
  • συμφέρων (sumpheron) = gain, advantage
  • σωφροσύνη (sophrosune) = moderation, mindfulness (not "temperance") [opposed to ἀφροσύνη or ἀκολασία]
  • τέλος (telos) = goal, completeness
  • φαῦλος (phaulos) = unworthy, trivial, bad [opposed to σπουδαῖος]
  • φρόνησις (phronesis) = wisdom, good judgment (not "prudence")
  • χρῆσις (chresis) = use, employment, application
  • ψυχή (psuche) = aliveness (not "soul")
