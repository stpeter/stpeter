Peter Saint-Andre: Writings

My main project as a writer is a six-movement suite of short books on how to live a good life, composed as reflections on Ayn Rand (The Tao of Roark), Friedrich Nietzsche, Aristotle, Henry David Thoreau, Epicurus (Letters on Happiness), and Lao Tzu.

I have also written a dictionary of philosophy, a book of poems, numerous essays, and various translations from Greek, Latin, and German (not counting my technical publications).

I have made all of these works available in the public domain, and have also started to publish them in paperback and ebook formats via the Monadnock Valley Press. Further information is available at the links below.

  • Letters on Happiness: An Epicurean Dialogue (2013)
  • The Tao of Roark: Variations on a Theme from Ayn Rand (2012)
  • Randian Reflections (2009)
  • Ancient Fire: Poems and Translations (2000)
  • The Ism Book: A Field Guide to Philosophy (1996)

I'm currently working on four projects:

  • The Upland Farm: Thoreau on Cultivating a Better Life
  • Songs of Zarathustra: Poetic Perspectives on Nietzsche's Ethics
  • Epicurus on the Good Life: A Fresh Translation
  • A Worthy Life: The Essence of Aristotle's Ethics

Eventually I might also write a book about Taoism, even though I know that the Tao Te Ching cannot be improved upon.
