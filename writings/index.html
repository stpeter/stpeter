Peter Saint-Andre: Writings

My main project as a writer is a six-movement suite of short books on how to live a good life, composed as reflections on the following thinkers:

  1. Ayn Rand (The Tao of Roark, 2012)
  2. Friedrich Nietzsche (Songs of Zarathustra, in progress)
  3. Aristotle (A Worthy Life, in progress)
  4. Henry David Thoreau (The Upland Farm, in progress)
  5. Epicurus (Letters on Happiness, 2013)
  6. Lao Tzu

I have also written a short dictionary of philosophy (The Ism Book, 1996), a book of poems and translations (Ancient Fire: Poems and Translations, 2000), numerous essays (most collected into Randian Reflections, 2009), and a fresh translation of the ethical writings of Epicurus.

I have made all of these works available in the public domain, and have also started to publish them in paperback and ebook formats via the Monadnock Valley Press.
