The Upland Farm: Thoreau on Cultivating a Good Life

by Peter Saint-Andre

Last Updated: November 27, 2015

This a rough outline of the book, divided (as Walden is) into 18 chapters.

  1. Site
  2. Seed
  3. Preparing
  4. Sprout
  5. Trust
  6. Planting
  7. Tree
  8. Cultivating
  9. Flower
  10. Magnanimity
  11. Fruit
  12. Harvesting
  13. Independence
  14. Leaf
  15. Storing
  16. Simplicity
  17. Bud
  18. Offering

1. Site

  • "allow to be in a place"
  • farm = having firm control over
  • firm = fixed, established, solid, strong
  • quote from letter to Blake
  • higher possibilities
  • individual (footpath)
  • no shortcuts or mechanical aids
  • pack of the upland farmer (few tools)
  • seed, sprout, flower, fruit, leaf, bud - natural expressions of one's human potential that must be actively cultivated
  • cultivation as working with nature, tension inherent
  • Varro's seasons: preparing, planting, cultivating, harvesting, storing, offering for sale

2. Seed

  • "that which is sowed"
  • related to season and secular
  • aspiration
  • awareness of higher possibilities
  • crops of the upland farm: arbor vitae, apples of the Hesperides, life everlasting
  • choosing to pursue a higher life
  • reap what you sow, so choose well

3. Preparing

  • "making ready"
  • upland farmer has only a small pack: no horses or tractors or plows or greenhouses etc.; work with your hands and take advantage of nature
  • tools (base meaning "to make with the hands, to do or create")
  • observation / alertness
  • sincerity / honesty with yourself
  • patience
  • discipline / stoicism
  • diligence / persistence
  • studiousness
  • vigor / strength / endurance
  • focus
  • curiosity

4. Sprout

  • "a young twig or spray"
  • nurturing / tenderness
  • indoor growth before exposure and planting
  • but no greenhouse at the upland farm so need to find a sheltered spot facing south
  • raised beds
  • risk of late frosts
  • dodging of pressures / Bourne

5. Trust

  • "as firm and straight as a tree"
  • also faithful, loyal, troth=pledged to fidelity
  • self trust / Emerson
  • trust in nature
  • trust in future
  • trust in others / friendship
  • loyalty to a life of principle (loyal related to legal)
  • being worthy of trust
  • faith in a seed (trust / confidence)
  • crown of the spring virtues
  • describe other spring virtues
  • hope
  • confidence
  • solidity
  • formation of character
  • emergence of self
  • identity
  • steadfastness
  • elasticity
  • truth
  • vernal / veracity
  • seeming opposite of independence?

6. Planting

  • "to place into the ground to start growing"
  • plow / till / turn over
  • receptivity / softening
  • at home in and on the earth
  • digging down / spadework
  • first roots
  • tender growth
  • nutriment from the soil

7. Tree

  • just "tree" - a primary substance?
  • rooting downward
  • branching / reaching upward
  • solidity / steadfastness
  • tree of life
  • or: of the knowledge of good and evil
  • growth upward into a more rarified air
  • trees don't grow to the sky - but try to reach as high as one is able
  • shading those beneath you

8. Cultivating

  • "active care"
  • watering with attention / lavish
  • weeding out distractions
  • pruning your hopes
  • water, light, air for the soul
  • enlightenment
  • carrying water / pail
  • pulling / cutting / shears
  • trimming / saw

9. Flower

  • "to bloom, to flourish"
  • beauty, not use
  • but flowers are useful, too
  • pollination
  • nectar
  • bees and butterflies
  • being an inspiration to those around you
  • making honey

10. Magnanimity

  • "great hearted, high minded"
  • courage to reach higher (opposite is pusillanimous = lacking courage)
  • noble pursuits
  • outdoor life that is all for action
  • great noon
  • crown of the summer virtues
  • describe other summer virtues
  • influence of Aristotle / Greeks
  • big heart (core)
  • high mind (essence of one's person)
  • flowering / flourishing
  • pollination
  • seeming opposite of simplicity?

11. Fruit

  • "full use and enjoyment, successful realization"
  • a free gift of oneself
  • coming to fruition
  • realizing one's potential
  • the apple of the tree of knowledge
  • but beauty, not use

12. Harvesting

  • akin to "plucking" (Latin carpere cf. carpe diem)
  • ripe = "ready to be cut"
  • reaping what you have sowed
  • a late and perfect maturity
  • testing for ripeness / observing oneself
  • self knowledge
  • gathering together
  • festivals, celebrations
  • but an individual harvest

13. Independence

  • "not deriving from or relying on"
  • a more individual existence
  • become what you are
  • character
  • crown of the fall virtues
  • describe other fall virtues
  • influence of romantics (Goethe, Coleridge)
  • become who you are
  • one's true character
  • ripeness
  • maturity
  • color
  • nuttiness
  • seeming opposite of trust?

14. Leaf

  • "what is peeled or stripped off"
  • what you leave behind
  • spiritual inheritance
  • Iliad quote

15. Storing

  • "keeping for future use"
  • "storage" through writing
  • removing the chaff
  • cooling and drying / reflection
  • a record of one's strivings and learnings
  • footprints for others to follow (cf. footpath of upland farm)

16. Simplicity

  • "something with one fold"
  • an unfolding of oneself
  • also ply = to use, wield, practice consistently or diligently
  • ply a single heavenly trade
  • focus on essentials
  • not for the sake of simplicity itself, but clearing the ground for something higher
  • crown of the winter virtues
  • describe other winter virtuas as well
  • influence of Stoics (and Hinduism?)
  • dispersion of seeds
  • dormancy in all its meanings
  • seeming opposite of magnanimity?

17. Bud

  • "swelling", "extension"
  • pregnant with possibilities
  • the buds and hidden shoots of winter
  • being ever green and full of sap
  • second spring

18. Offering

  • "presenting, giving, sacrificing"
  • offering oneself to what is highest
  • transcend = "to climb above"
  • to spend as a produce is to become spent (Shaw quote)
  • private ecstasy above even the farm
