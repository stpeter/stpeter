A Dialogue between the Wanderer and His Shadow

by Peter Saint-Andre

WANDERER
My shadow! Aren't you of my past,
That old, unreasoned dark of mine,
My weaknesses, my vanity?

SHADOW
I disagree: I'm only cast
Whenever suns of knowledge shine.
I'm insight, not insanity.

WANDERER
If you bring light, then why so dark? I need the shade of knowledge less Than positive success in action.

SHADOW
The joy of knowledge has its arc: It doesn't bring full happiness, And yet it does yield satisfaction.

(Human, All Too Human, Volume II, Part III, introduction)
