Songs of Zarathustra

Suggestions for Further Exploration

As is true of aphorisms (see Nietzsche's preface to On the Genealogy of Morals), so also of poems: they require exegesis. Because these poems condense my perspective on Nietzsche into verse, the best places to start are the passages referenced at the end of each poem. Naturally, these passages comprise the merest slice of what he wrote; to check my interpretations with due skepticism, one can do no better than to read his books, preferably in chronological order.

(Note: A future revision of this page will provide suggestions among the vast secondary literature on Nietzsche's life and thought.)

Songs of Zarathustra is the second volume in a six-movement "suite" of books I'm writing on the art of living:

  1. The Tao of Roark: Variations on a Theme from Ayn Rand
  2. Songs of Zarathustra: Poetic Perspectives on Nietzsche's Philosophy of Life
  3. A Worthy Life: The Essence of Aristotle's Ethics
  4. The Upland Farm: Thoreau on Cultivating a Better Life
  5. Letters on Happiness: An Epicurean Dialogue
  6. Stories of the Way: Parables on Taoism
