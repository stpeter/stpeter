The Lightning Strike

by Peter Saint-Andre

In January 1889,
A bolt of lightning struck a lonely pine.
His mind, which always shone so bright, went dark.
The fire blew out, but left an ageless spark.

(cf. Nietzsche's poems "Lonely, Through the Dark Blue" and "Pine and Lightning")

[On January 3rd, 1889, Nietzsche experienced a mental collapse into insanity (likely caused by a brain disease, not syphilis as often believed); he never recovered.]
