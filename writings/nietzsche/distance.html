A Healthy Distance

by Peter Saint-Andre

To pity is to view
The things befalling you
As if I had to share
In them, as if I care
Here as I do for me.
My objectivity
Is better served if I
Can view them with the eye
Of far eternity.
Applying this to me
As well, I recognize
The true and tiny size
Of my own small concerns;
Indeed the wise man learns
To see what's dear as near
And far. I do not fear
The pale and colder sight
I gain by distant light.
