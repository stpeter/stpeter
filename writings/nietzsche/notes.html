Notes Toward Songs of Zarathustra

Mistral Wind

In old Provence there is a gale So powerful they call it master — A rain- and cloud- and darkness-blaster That sets all gloom away to sail

Far off across the southern ocean. This clarity of light and air Inspires the singers' gai saber: The artful and extreme devotion

To learning of a joyous way Of life.
