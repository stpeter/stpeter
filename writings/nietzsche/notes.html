Notes Toward Songs of Zarathustra

Schopenhauer as Educator

Education and philosophy for life - to be a free person, to cultivate and liberate yourself, to follow your genius, to be unconditionally honest and simple and true in thought and life, to be beautifully consistent in your uniqueness, to live according to your own laws and standards, to be responsible to yourself, to be the true helmsman of your existence, to honor what you have truly loved and revered, what has borne your soul aloft into its higher nature.

To be an example in your outward life, in how you bear yourself, how you dress, how you eat, how you speak - in all the practicalities of living - to live a visible philosophical life.

How to derive a circle of duties and a flow of life from this ideal? Promote the philosopher and artist and saint within yourself, and also in others. Set yourself goals that transcend money-making, service to state and society, superficial beauty or conventional virtue or dry knowledge. Cultivate wisdom, holiness, love, and longing for what is best and rare and great and uncommon in human existence. To live in accordance with and for the sake of this outer and inner philosophical life. Not the platitudes of ethics, but its living, heroic reality, informing all your deeds and pursuits.

***

Waiting for some savior - a thinker, artist, saint -
Lacks the active principle of life and has the taint
Of slothfulness regarding the improvement of my mind
And soul. The goal, instead, is actively to seek and find

The path that only I can walk, the bridge that only I can cross,
The higher law that helps me soar so far above the dross
Of the customs of these times that I must freely give
The life-enhancing gift of how and why I live,

The beautiful consistency of all my deeds.
The longing for what is great and rare, which feeds
The flow of life and founds the laws by which I act,
Teaches me to cultivate the natural fact

Of what I am. Striving always to transcend
The common run ...
