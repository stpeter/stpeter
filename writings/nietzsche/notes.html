Notes Toward Songs of Zarathustra

At the Feet of Dionysus

(BGE 295)

Long have I sat at the feet of this hidden, tempter god and philosopher
Yet his wisdom is very far from the popular slander against Dionysus...
For he entices me to:
turn inward
nurture a thorough devotion
treasure silence
listen quietly
question myself
lie still and mirror the deep sky
hesitate in my actions
grasp things less greedily
soak in sweet spirituality
be richer, not in things, but in myself
be ever new and newer to myself
be full of unnamed hopes, new will, new energy
be more courageous, more alone, more profound, more beautiful
In these ways is he not a liberator who delivers me from care?
