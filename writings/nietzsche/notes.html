Notes Toward Songs of Zarathustra

You say you know which course is best?
Then let us put it to the test!
The action will prove false or true
If we but give the trial its due.
But if you say experiment
Won't yield an answer, then we've spent
Our energy on fruitless chatter
That never can decide the matter.

I look back gently on the time
When I had every answer.
From there I've made a steady climb,
Light-footed as a dancer,
On mountain paths toward starry heights
Where certainties are few.
The perils of these lonely flights
Will yield up what is true.
