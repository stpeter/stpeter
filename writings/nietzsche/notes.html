Notes Toward Songs of Zarathustra

Midnight Song

  • Solitude, and a homecoming in solitude, blissful and tender and still
  • Inquiring, seeking, overcoming
  • Something unquenched and unquenchable is in me
  • It is night and I am light
  • The fountains of delight speak louder
  • Alone I live in my own light
  • Giving, not receiving
  • The moon's love affair: pale, pure, reflected love in knowledge only, not golden and warming and blessing like the light of the sun
  • The insubstantial images and gods and overmen of the poets - pale and wraithlike compared to life itself
  • The dew falls on the grass when the night is most silent
  • A realization that you experience only yourself
  • Treading your path of greatness
  • Climbing above yourself
  • Luminous sky, stars, wisdom - cold and pale but above the clouds of confusion and a sacred No - instead a Yes and Amen
  • The bell rings twelve times, and one thinks of leaving life - but life knows that one loves her more than wisdom
  • Pain is also joy, a curse is also a blessing, the night is also a sun, midnight is also a noon

At midnight comes a second noon,
When time again stands still.

The Dawn

  • Between dawn and dawn comes a new truth
  • Working and making company with those who create and harvest and rejoice
  • Begetters and culivators and sowers of the future
  • Loving the earth as a creator and begetter
  • A new morning, a new nobility
  • This is my day, my dawn - rise up!
  • Joy at a new existence
  • The will as its own redeemer and bringer of joy
  • Every soul is a world of its own
  • Forming the world in your image by your reason, will, and love
  • Your work, your will, your love
  • To step beyond what has been merely human until now
  • BGE 271: an ardor and thirst that drives the soul out of night into morning, out of gloom into brightness, a noble inclination toward what is glittering, profound, refined
  • The glowing love of the sun at dawn
  • Sun-love as innocence and creative desire
  • Not wisdom but life
  • A little wisdom is possible, but only a little!
  • There is too much of chance in life to gain much certainty - this in itself brings a happy certainty, to dance on feet of chance
  • To learn and know only for the sake of creating
  • Offering the honey of wisdom for the sake of your work - casting your happiness as a lure for others
  • Affirmation of life despite its pains
