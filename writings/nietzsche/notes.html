Notes Toward Songs of Zarathustra

Aubade

  • Awakening to life
  • Enlightenment
  • Planting the seed of your highest hope (cf twilight)
  • A new beginning
  • Creating new values (cf Dawn)
  • Creating freedom
  • A sacred Yes
  • Honesty as the youngest virtue
  • Purity and cleanliness of the body
  • The body as a great intelligence
  • Not saying I but performing I
  • Creating beyond yourself
  • Purify yourself, even if you are a free spirit
  • Keep holy your highest hope
  • The earth remains free for great souls
  • Greatness is individual creativity
  • Creation as seeking a way to yourself
  • The individual is the latest, best creation
  • Consecrating the I
  • A new strength, a new right
  • A first motion, a self-propelling wheel
  • Your own will as a law above your soul
  • Mystery before noon
  • Morning dream and awakening day-wisdom: weighing the world in a pair scales - a good, human thing is the world, a garden of joy, an earthly heavens
  • The self-rejoicing soul - a sound and healthy selfishness - to love oneself with a sound and healthy love
  • Banishing all that is low and contemptible, all worrying and sighing and complaining
  • Not a commandment but a fine, subtle, patient art
  • To wait for yourself and your own way

The Great Noon

  • Remaining true to the earth
  • Not seeking first beyond the stars
  • Creating meaning for the earth
  • Living for knowledge
  • Working and inventing
  • Loving virtue
  • Having a deep, lavish, overfull soul
  • Justifying the men of the future
  • Having a free spirit and a free heart
  • Pride and wisdom (eagle and serpent)
  • Being halfway between animal and the more-than-human
  • Halfway on a journey through evening to a new morning
  • The sun of knowledge stands still at noon
  • A moment out of time
  • The summer solstice of the day
  • A way to a new dawn
  • Being ready and ripe
  • The world become perfect, the world itself grown ripe
  • Resting close to the earth - faithful, trusting, waiting - fastened by the finest threads
  • The best happiness comes from the smallest, gentlest, lightest things
  • The earth is rich in good little perfect things, in well constitued things, which teach hope and heal the heart
  • How much is still possible!
  • It is good to live on the earth, to will and love and desire the kingdom of the earth

Twilight Dance

  • Highest hope - the journey to a new morning
  • Life is a fountain of delight
  • Life: not unfathomable, but changeable and untamed/defiant
  • Praising life because I will, desire, love
  • Loving life but fond of wisdom
  • The two are very much alike!
  • Thirsting for wisdom but never satisfied
  • Perhaps she too is wicked and false?
  • Even though life is changeable, my will is invulnerable, unburiable, unchanging; thus do I overcome the pains of life and the death of my highest hopes
  • The inexhaustible, ever begetting life-will; a will to self-command and to not be remiss in obeying my highest commands; a will to create and procreate beyond myself; a will to higher, more distant, more manifold goals
  • Life and wisdom are the loves I choose between

Midnight Song

  • Solitude, and a homecoming in solitude, blissful and tender and still
  • Inquiring, seeking, overcoming
  • Something unquenched and unquenchable is in me
  • It is night and I am light
  • The fountains of delight speak louder
  • Alone I live in my own light
  • Giving, not receiving
  • The moon's love affair: pale, pure, reflected love in knowledge only, not golden and warming and blessing like the light of the sun
  • The insubstantial images and gods and overmen of the poets - pale and wraithlike compared to life itself
  • The dew falls on the grass when the night is most silent
  • A realization that you experience only yourself
  • Treading your path of greatness
  • Climbing above yourself
  • Luminous sky, stars, wisdom - cold and pale but above the clouds of confusion and a sacred No - instead a Yes and Amen
  • The bell rings twelve times, and one thinks of leaving life - but life knows that one loves her more than wisdom
  • Pain is also joy, a curse is also a blessing, the night is also a sun, midnight is also a noon

At midnight comes a second noon,
When time again stands still.

The Dawn

  • Between dawn and dawn comes a new truth
  • Working and making company with those who create and harvest and rejoice
  • Begetters and culivators and sowers of the future
  • Loving the earth as a creator and begetter
  • A new morning, a new nobility
  • This is my day, my dawn - rise up!
  • Joy at a new existence
  • The will as its own redeemer and bringer of joy
  • Every soul is a world of its own
  • Forming the world in your image by your reason, will, and love
  • Your work, your will, your love
  • To step beyond what has been merely human until now
  • BGE 271: an ardor and thirst that drives the soul out of night into morning, out of gloom into brightness, a noble inclination toward what is glittering, profound, refined
  • The glowing love of the sun at dawn
  • Sun-love as innocence and creative desire
  • Not wisdom but life
  • A little wisdom is possible, but only a little!
  • There is too much of chance in life to gain much certainty - this in itself brings a happy certainty, to dance on feet of chance
  • To learn and know only for the sake of creating
  • Offering the honey of wisdom for the sake of your work - casting your happiness as a lure for others
  • Affirmation of life despite its pains
